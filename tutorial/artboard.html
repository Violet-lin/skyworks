Artboard to Group layers


How to convert Photoshop files in Artboard format to regular Group layers (Manually)
Sometimes you need to export elements easily without Artboard,
but they just does not work the way you want them to be.
1. If you have any locked layers in the files, select all layers and press cmd+/ twice.
By pressing cmd+/ twice, it enables all layers to be unlocked.
2a. Select all Artboard,

2b. Select Move Tool(V).

• If the artboards are in horizontal format, click either Align left edges or Align right edges.



• If the artboards are in vertical format, click either Align top edges or Align bottom edges instead.



This should combine the artboards into one.
You can choose to work from here :)
Or if you prefer to work in groups, continue to Step 3.
3. Group cmd+G all layers within Artboard 1.

4. Go on to group Artboard 2, Artboard 3 and so on.
5. After you have complete all Artboard, you should have 1 group in each Artboard.

6. cmd+a and select Add layer mask on group 1.

7. Select all layers and choose Ungroup Artboards..

8. Keep only the Group 1 open and hide the rest of the Groups.

9. Image > Trim

10. All sides.

11. Completed. There you have a psd file without Artboard function.
